<p><p>Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension (PAH) is a progressive condition characterized by elevated blood pressure in the pulmonary arteries, leading to heart failure if untreated. The market for PAH treatments includes several classes: Prostacyclin and its analogs, which improve blood flow and exhibit vasodilatory effects; soluble guanylate cyclase (SGC) stimulators that enhance nitric oxide signaling; endothelin receptor antagonists (ERA) which block endothelin-1 pathways; and phosphodiesterase-5 (PDE-5) inhibitors that promote vasodilation. Each category plays a unique role in managing PAH symptoms and progression.</p></p>
